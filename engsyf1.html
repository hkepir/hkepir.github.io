The Life of Cahit Arf

Cahit Arf (October 24, 1910, Thessaloniki – December 26, 1997, Istanbul) was a Turkish mathematician and scientist. He made significant contributions to the fields of mathematics and science, developing terms such as the "Arf Invariant," "Arf Rings," and "Arf Closures," which are named after him. Additionally, he collaborated with German mathematician Helmut Hesse to develop the Hesse-Arf Theorem.

Education Life of Cahit Arf

Arf completed his higher education at École Normale Supérieure in France and worked as a mathematics teacher at Galatasaray High School. He later earned his doctorate at the University of Göttingen. Upon returning to Turkey, he became a professor and was later granted the title of Ordinary Professor at Istanbul University. He also taught at Middle East Technical University (METU) and served as the first president of TÜBİTAK's Scientific Board. Furthermore, he was the president of the Turkish Mathematical Society.

Arf was the recipient of prestigious awards, such as the İnönü Prize (1943) and the TÜBİTAK Science Award (1974). His famous quote, "Mathematics is fundamentally an event of patience. It must be understood not by memorizing but by discovering," reflects his approach to science and education.
